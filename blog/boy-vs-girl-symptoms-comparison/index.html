Deep Dive: Why Do These Myths Exist?

While modern science tells us that gender is determined by the X or Y chromosome from the father, these myths often have a small "grain" of truth. For example, studies have shown that women carrying girls often have higher levels of the hormone hCG, which is directly linked to more severe morning sickness.

The "Sweet vs. Salty" Cravings

It is said that "Boys are made of snakes and snails and puppy-dog tails" (or in this case, meat and salt!), while "Girls are made of sugar and spice and everything nice." If you find yourself reaching for the salt shaker more than the chocolate bar, traditionalists will bet on a boy every time.

Bump Shape and Position

Carrying "all in the front"? This is the classic boy sign. The idea is that boys are carried like a ball tucked under the shirt, whereas girls cause the weight to spread out more around the waist and hips.
