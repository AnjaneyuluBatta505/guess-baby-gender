1. Identify Your "Fertile Window"

You are only fertile for about 5 to 6 days each month. This window includes the day you ovulate and the few days leading up to it. Sperm can live inside the female body for up to 5 days, but an egg only lives for 12-24 hours after release. This is why tracking your ovulation is the most important step in the planning phase.

3. Monitor Your Basal Body Temperature (BBT)

Your body temperature rises slightly (about 0.5 to 1 degree) right after ovulation occurs. By tracking your temperature every morning before you get out of bed, you can begin to see patterns in your cycle, helping you predict when you'll be most fertile next month.
